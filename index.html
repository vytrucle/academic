Truc Vy Le

I am a psychology graduate from Vietnam. I graduated from Ho Chi Minh City University of Education, Vietnam, with a B.A. in Psychology with very good distinction. I have four years of experience working as a research assistant at the TCVL Lab under the supervision of Dr. Vinh-Long Tran-Chi, where I have been involved in psychological and mental health research projects, especially research on adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) and mental health.

In addition to my research experience, I have two years of clinical experience in psychological assessment and counseling. My work has included supporting incarcerated individuals as well as inpatient and outpatient populations experiencing anxiety, depression, and stress-related difficulties, alongside training in psychological assessment tools and cognitive-behavioral counseling practice.

My long-term goal is to become a clinical neuropsychologist. My research interests focus on adverse childhood experiences (ACEs), cognitive functioning, and the neuropsychological mechanisms underlying the relationship between early adversity and mental health.
